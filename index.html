Literature Survey

Visually impaired individuals face significant challenges in accessing digital content, primarily due to the lack of essential accessibility features such as screen reader compatibility, keyboard navigation, and alternative input methods. According to Lazar et al. [1], these barriers severely restrict educational and employment opportunities for visually impaired users, as many web applications fail to accommodate their needs. The absence of these features significantly limits their ability to interact with online platforms, highlighting the critical need for systems that prioritize accessibility and inclusivity.


Educational platforms, in particular, often do not adhere to accessibility guidelines, which places visually impaired students at a substantial disadvantage. Kelly and Smith [2] found that many educational technologies lack the necessary accommodations to provide equitable access, leading to a significant learning gap for these students. They emphasize the importance of integrating comprehensive accessibility features such as screen readers and keyboard navigation to ensure that visually impaired learners can fully engage with educational content. This underscores the necessity for platforms like VisionPath that are designed with the specific needs of visually impaired users in mind, thereby promoting an inclusive educational environment.

In the job market, visually impaired individuals also face considerable difficulties, with most job portals lacking support for alternative input methods, which makes it challenging for visually impaired job seekers to independently search for and apply to jobs [3]. Thompson and Brough’s research highlights the urgent need for accessible job search tools that cater to the specific needs of visually impaired users. The American Foundation for the Blind [4] stresses the importance of developing digital platforms that foster socio-economic inclusion by integrating comprehensive accessibility features. VisionPath aims to address these gaps by offering tools such as personalized job recommendations, emotion detection during online courses, and features like screen readers and keyboard navigation, thus promoting greater inclusion and empowerment for visually impaired individuals.

Research Problem & Solution

Research Problem

To effectively tackel the central research problem its crucial to adopt a multi faced approach that integrates technical innovation, accessibility enhancements, and user-centered design principles. This comprehensive strategy comprises several vital components. Firstly, technology advancement is key to developing an integrated job-seeking and training application. This involves seamlessly incorporating advanced features like speech-to-text, text-to-speech, natural language processing (NLP), and virtual interview/training sessions. Achieving this requires careful selection of suitable APIs or developing custom solutions to ensure accurate and smooth conversion between speech and text, as well as extracting meaningful insights from textual data. Concurrently, ensuring accessibility for individuals with vision impairments is paramount. It entails strict adherence to established accessibility standards such as WCAG and implementing features like screen reader compatibility, keyboard navigation, high contrast modes, and alternative text for images. Additionally, providing customizable settings to cater to various levels of visual impairment and individual user preferences is essential to foster inclusivity and usability.Furthermore, adopting a user-centered design approach is critical in shaping the application's interface and functionality to meet the specific needs and preferences of users with vision impairments. Conducting thorough user research and usability testing within this demographic enables a deep understanding of their unique challenges, preferences, and pain points. This insight informs the creation of an intuitive, easy-to-navigate interface optimized for accessibility, while offering multiple interaction modalities such as voice commands and gestures to enhance the overall user experience. Moreover, the application should offer a comprehensive array of resources and tools for real-world interview preparation. This encompasses features like mock interview simulations, industry-specific practice questions, and guidance on effective communication and presentation skills. Access to informational resources such as resume/CV writing guides and career development articles further empowers users in their job-seeking endeavors.

Solution

VisionPath is a comprehensive web application designed to bridge the accessibility gap for visually impaired users. Our platform offers a range of tools, including accessible educational resources, personalized job recommendations, and an intuitive CV builder. By integrating advanced technologies like AI, machine learning, and emotion detection, VisionPath provides a seamless and inclusive user experience that empowers visually impaired individuals to achieve their educational and career goals. Join us at VisionPath and experience a platform tailored to your unique needs, promoting greater socio-economic inclusion and personal empowerment.

Research Objectives

The research goals we sought to accomplish with this study are listed below.

LEARNING WEB APP FOR VISUALLY IMPAIRED, TRACKS UNDERSTANDING VIA FACIAL EXPRESSIONS

Sub Objectives

  1. Develop a personalized career guidance system to recommend suitable career pathways for Sri Lankan university students based on their qualifications and skills.
  2. Implement a salary prediction mechanism for each recommended career path, considering candidate details such as qualifications, skills, and geographical factors.
  3. Create a job market opportunity prediction model to forecast the demand and growth of chosen career paths over the next 5 years, utilizing data from job listings and industry reports.
  4. Utilize machine learning to identify areas where candidates need to further develop their skills, providing comprehensive career guidance and recommendations.

DEVELOP WEB APP HELPS BLIND INDIVIDUALS IN JOB-SEEKING AND CAREER DEVELOPMENT

Sub Objectives

  1. Automated Application Processing
  2. Personality Evaluation Integration
  3. Dynamic Advertisement Dissemination
  4. Customized Social Networking Platform

USE NLP TO CRAFT QUALITY RESUMES WITH RELEVANT KEYWORDS

Sub Objectives

  1. Automate CV Evaluation: Develop machine learning algorithms to automatically extract and evaluate relevant information from CVs, enabling efficient candidate screening.
  2. Standardize Evaluation Criteria: Establish a structured rating system to objectively assess candidate suitability based on predefined job requirements and qualifications.
  3. Enhance Candidate Matching: Compare CVs against specific job criteria to identify the best-fit candidates and minimize mismatches between applicant qualifications and job roles.
  4. Streamline Interview Scheduling: Implement automated scheduling processes to efficiently coordinate interviews for qualified candidates, reducing administrative burden and improving time-to-hire metrics.
  5. Improve Recruitment Efficiency: By automating key aspects of the recruitment process, aim to reduce manual effort, enhance evaluation accuracy, and expedite candidate selection.

LEVERAGED VIRTUAL INTERVIEWS FOR EFFECTIVE ASSESSMENT AND  EVALUATION

Sub Objectives

  1. Develop a machine learning-powered web service for real-time assessment of source code quality and syntax accuracy.
  2. Implement a grammar checking mechanism to assign scores to individual users based on their code quality.
  3. Create a user activity monitoring feature to ensure solitary participation during assessment sessions.
  4. Explore the application of the system in candidate valuation for recruitment processes by skillfully assessing coding proficiency through machine learning and real-time evaluation.

Methodology

The complete proposed system for this project has four main components namely,
  1. 1. LEARNING WEB APP FOR VISUALLY IMPAIRED, TRACKS UNDERSTANDING VIA FACIAL EXPRESSIONS
  2. 2. DEVELOP WEB APP HELPS BLIND INDIVIDUALS IN JOB-SEEKING AND CAREER DEVELOPMENT
  3. 3. USE NLP TO CRAFT QUALITY RESUMES WITH RELEVANT KEYWORDS
  4. 4. LEVERAGED VIRTUAL INTERVIEWS FOR EFFECTIVE ASSESSMENT AND  EVALUATION

AA user starts by logging into the website and filling out their profile details, including education, skills, and career interests. Once the profile is completed, the system recommends two job roles with salary predictions tailored to the user's background. Additionally, it provides job opportunity predictions, highlighting the most promising specializations for the next five years, and identifies any skills gaps for the predicted job roles, offering suggestions for improvement.

For personalized job recommendations, organizations can add job posts by uploading detailed descriptions. The system extracts relevant information from these posts and matches them with users’ CV skills, recommending suitable jobs. Candidates can upload their CVs, browse job listings, and search for specific positions. The system displays matching job posts along with a compatibility rating, indicating how well the user's CV aligns with each job.

In the recruitment optimization component, candidates log into their profiles and upload their CVs. The system automatically extracts details from the CVs and updates their profiles. Organizations manage job posts, view applications, and see ranked CVs that match their requirements. Recruiters can then schedule interviews and send emails directly to the top candidates through the system.

Lastly, for self-assessment and skill evaluation, users can start an interview simulation that mimics a real technical job interview. Candidates receive Python programming problems to solve, and their answers are evaluated based on code modularity, style, comment density, consistency, and input-output correctness. After completing the simulation, users receive a detailed evaluation of their performance, highlighting their strengths and areas for improvement.
